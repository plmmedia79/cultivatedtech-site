1. Scope

This Privacy Policy (the "Policy") explains how Cultivated Technology Group, Inc. ("Cultivated Technology Group," "the Firm," "we," "us," or "our"), a California General Stock Corporation (California Secretary of State Entity Number 4701299) with its registered office at 401 W Fallbrook, Suite 205, Fresno, CA 93711, United States, collects, uses, discloses, and otherwise processes personal information about visitors to our website, prospective clients, current clients, and individuals associated with them.

This Policy applies to personal information processed in connection with: (i) our public-facing website and any subdomain operated by the Firm; (ii) communications you initiate with us by email, telephone, web form, or otherwise; (iii) prospective and ongoing client engagements; and (iv) any business records the Firm maintains in the ordinary course of operating as a California corporation.

This Policy does not apply to personal information that we process on behalf of our clients in the course of delivering professional services to them. Where we act as a service provider, processor, or sub-processor for a client, the client's own privacy policy and any applicable data-processing agreement will govern that processing, and we will process such information only in accordance with the client's documented instructions.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information through several channels. The categories of personal information we process include:

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Contact form submissions. When you complete a contact form on our website, we collect the information you provide — including your full name, business email address, company name, country, and the project description you write. Submitting this information is voluntary, but the form will not function if required fields are left blank.
  • Email and telephone correspondence. When you contact us by email, telephone, or other communication channel, we receive the content of your message and the metadata associated with it (such as sender address and timestamps).
  • Engagement materials. If we enter into a professional engagement, we receive the information necessary to perform the engagement — typically including the names, titles, and contact details of stakeholders on your side, and any materials you choose to share in connection with the project.
  • Onboarding and verification. For new business clients, we collect limited information for Know-Your-Business (KYB) onboarding, which may include the client entity's legal name and registered address, an authorized signatory's name and role, and a supporting document confirming the entity's existence.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Server logs. Our web server records standard log entries for each request, including IP address, user-agent string, the page or asset requested, response status, and timestamp. These logs are used for security, troubleshooting, and capacity planning.
  • Cookies and similar technologies. See Section 5 below.
  • Analytics. We may use privacy-respecting analytics tooling that records aggregated visit counts and basic interaction metrics. We do not use analytics tools that build cross-site advertising profiles of visitors.

2.3 Information We Do Not Collect

We do not knowingly collect, request, or have any business need for sensitive categories of personal information — including government identifiers, financial account numbers, biometric data, health data, or precise geolocation — from visitors to our website. Where an engagement requires us to handle such information for a client, the controls applicable to that engagement are set out in a written data-processing agreement.

3. Purposes of Processing

We process personal information for the following purposes:

  • To respond to inquiries you submit to us through the contact form, by email, or by telephone, and to follow up on those inquiries as appropriate.
  • To enter into and perform professional services engagements, including discovery, scoping, project delivery, invoicing, and ongoing support.
  • To comply with our legal and regulatory obligations as a California corporation, including tax, accounting, and record-keeping requirements.
  • To operate, secure, and improve our website and internal systems, including detecting and preventing fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access.
  • To send transactional communications about active engagements, and — only with your consent and only where appropriate — occasional business communications such as policy updates.
  • To establish, exercise, or defend legal claims where necessary.

We do not sell personal information, and we do not use personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising or for any form of automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects on the individual.

4. Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR)

Where the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR"), the United Kingdom's Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR, or an equivalent regional law applies to our processing of your personal information, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract — where the processing is necessary to perform a contract with you or to take steps at your request prior to entering into a contract (for example, responding to a request for proposal).
  • Legitimate interests — where the processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests (such as operating and securing our website, communicating with prospective clients, or maintaining business records), provided those interests are not overridden by your rights or interests.
  • Compliance with a legal obligation — where the processing is necessary to comply with an obligation we are subject to under California, US federal, or other applicable law.
  • Consent — where you have given us specific, informed consent to a particular processing activity, for example by opting into non-essential cookies or marketing communications. You may withdraw consent at any time.

5. Cookies & Tracking

A "cookie" is a small text file that a website may store on your device. We use cookies and similar technologies sparingly. Our website may set the following categories:

  • Strictly necessary cookies required for the website to function — for example, a session token used while you are completing the contact form. These cookies do not require consent under applicable law.
  • Preference cookies that remember non-essential choices (such as a dark-mode preference) you make while using the site.
  • Analytics cookies set, where applicable, by a privacy-respecting analytics tool. These cookies do not identify you personally and are not used to build cross-site advertising profiles.

You can configure your browser to refuse or alert you to cookies. Most browsers also offer a "Do Not Track" signal; we honor a clear opt-out signal where it is technically feasible and required by law (including the Global Privacy Control where applicable). Disabling strictly necessary cookies may cause parts of the site to malfunction.

6. Data Sharing & Sub-Processors

We disclose personal information only in the limited circumstances described below.

  • Service providers and sub-processors. We use a small number of vendors to operate our business, including a website hosting provider, an email service provider, a productivity suite for internal collaboration, and an accounting service. We provide these vendors with the minimum information required for them to perform their services, and we contract with them to protect personal information in accordance with applicable law.
  • Professional advisors. We may disclose personal information to our legal counsel, accountants, auditors, and insurers, where reasonably necessary for the operation of the Firm.
  • Legal and regulatory requirements. We may disclose personal information where required by law, by valid legal process, or by a governmental or regulatory authority with jurisdiction over the Firm — including the California Secretary of State, the California Franchise Tax Board, and the United States Internal Revenue Service.
  • Business transfers. If the Firm is involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of business assets, personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to applicable law and customary confidentiality protections.

We do not sell or rent personal information to third parties, and we do not share personal information with advertising networks for targeted-advertising purposes.

7. International Transfers

The Firm is based in the United States. If you contact us from outside the United States, your personal information will be transferred to, stored in, and processed in the United States. Some of our service providers may also be located in jurisdictions other than the one in which you reside.

Where required by GDPR or equivalent law, we rely on appropriate safeguards for international transfers, including the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, or other lawful transfer mechanisms. You may request a copy of the safeguards applicable to a specific transfer by contacting us at the address in Section 13.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Policy and to comply with our legal, accounting, and contractual obligations.

  • Contact form inquiries are retained for up to 24 months from the date of last contact, then deleted or anonymized.
  • Engagement records are retained for the duration of the engagement and for a period of seven (7) years thereafter, consistent with statutory record-keeping requirements and the limitation periods that may apply to contractual claims.
  • Server logs are retained for up to 90 days, after which they are deleted or aggregated.

We will delete personal information sooner if you request deletion and we are not legally required to retain it.

9. Data Security

We maintain administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Measures include least-privilege access controls, encrypted-in-transit communications, encrypted-at-rest storage for sensitive systems, hardware-backed authentication for production access, regular dependency and security review of our internal tooling, and a documented incident-response procedure.

No method of electronic storage or transmission is perfectly secure. While we work to protect personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. If you believe your interaction with us is no longer secure, please contact us at the address in Section 13.

10. Your Rights

Depending on where you reside, you may have rights with respect to your personal information. These rights include, subject to applicable law and any applicable exceptions:

  • Right to access — to request confirmation of whether we process personal information about you, and a copy of that information.
  • Right to rectification — to request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — to request that we delete personal information about you, subject to retention obligations.
  • Right to portability — to receive personal information you have provided to us in a structured, machine-readable format, or to have it transmitted to another controller where technically feasible.
  • Right to object or restrict processing — to object to or restrict our processing of your personal information in certain circumstances, including processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • Right to lodge a complaint — with the supervisory authority in your country of residence.

California residents. Under the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA") as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act ("CPRA"), California residents have specific rights including the right to know, the right to delete, the right to correct, and the right to limit the use of sensitive personal information. California residents also have the right to direct a business not to sell or share their personal information. We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. California residents may exercise their rights, or designate an authorized agent to do so, by contacting us at the address in Section 13.

We will respond to verifiable rights requests within the timeframes required by applicable law and will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

11. Children's Privacy

Our services and our website are directed at business clients and the professionals associated with them. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of sixteen (16), and we do not direct our services to children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at the address in Section 13 and we will take steps to delete the information.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of the Policy and, where the changes are material, we will provide additional notice (such as an in-product notice or an email to active clients). We encourage you to review this Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your personal information.
