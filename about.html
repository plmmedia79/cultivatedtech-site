About the Firm

A small California firm,
built to last past the project.

Cultivated Technology Group, Inc. was incorporated in the State of California on February 16, 2021. We are headquartered in Fresno and we build reliable, maintainable software for businesses that intend to be around in a decade.

01 · Background

The firm was founded with a narrow thesis: a small group of senior engineers, working carefully on a small number of projects, can produce results that large consultancies and offshore shops cannot.

Five years in, that thesis still holds. We work with operators across the United States — companies that have outgrown their first piece of software, teams trying to retire a legacy system without taking the business down with it, founders who need to ship a credible v1 without spending eighteen months and seven hires getting there.

We do not chase scale. We do not staff junior engineers on client work. We do not subcontract delivery offshore. We answer our own phones, write our own code, and own the results — including the parts that don't go to plan.

02 · Mission, Vision, Values

Mission

To build software that holds up under the conditions of real business — long timelines, changing requirements, and people who weren't in the original meeting.

Vision

A small American engineering firm whose clients quietly compound for years afterward — because what was delivered did what it was supposed to do.

Values

Disciplined delivery, honest scoping, written reasoning, and a long view of every client relationship we accept.

03 · Leadership

Led by experienced engineering leadership.

The firm is led by its founder and principal engineer. Engagement leads are senior individual contributors with significant production experience.

John Edward Paul

Founder · Chief Executive Officer

John Edward Paul founded Cultivated Technology Group, Inc. in 2021 to build the kind of software firm he had spent his career looking for as a client. His work centers on disciplined delivery: clear scoping, careful estimation, and a written record of every decision a project depends on. He leads the firm's engineering practice and is personally involved in every active engagement.

His professional philosophy reflects a preference for craft over volume. He believes that software is a long-lived asset, that the day after launch is part of the project, and that clients are best served by engineers who can explain their reasoning in plain English. As Founder and Chief Executive Officer, he is responsible for the firm's commercial relationships and for ensuring that the work the firm accepts is the work the firm can finish well.
